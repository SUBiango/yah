About This Summit

A Generation Rising Together

The 3rd Kono District Youth Empowerment Summit was successfully held on Saturday, 15th November 2025, bringing together young people, community stakeholders, development actors, and young leaders to address key issues affecting youth in Kono District and Sierra Leone at large.

The summit attracted over 150 delegates, with strong representation from schools, youth-led organizations, and community groups across the district. With powerful keynote presentations, a highly engaging panel discussion on the rising abuse of Kush drug, interactive sessions, and open dialogue, the summit created a platform for learning, inspiration, and action.

The event also showcased the growing impact of Young Access Hub through its ongoing projects — ICT Skills Training Project, Annual Kono District Youth Empowerment Summit, and the EmpowerYouth Challenge Project.

Despite challenges related to limited sponsorship and tight timelines, the summit achieved its objectives and set the tone for an expanded, multi-day 4th edition in 2026.

The Programme

Day Highlights

Opening Keynote

Mohamed Samu opened the day with a powerful address on innovation, resilience, and the role of young people in shaping Sierra Leone's future.

Agriculture & Innovation Panel

A deep-dive panel on climate-smart agriculture and youth-led innovation, exploring how technology can transform livelihoods in Kono District.

Governance & Leadership Talk

Mayor Komba Sam and Hon. Rebecca Yei Sam shared their journeys in public service and urged young people to participate in civic life.

Entrepreneurship Masterclass

Tamba Songu Mbriwa led an interactive masterclass on building businesses with global reach, drawing from his own journey from Kono to the world stage.

Networking & Mentorship

Structured networking sessions gave attendees the chance to connect with speakers and peers, forging relationships and mentorships that outlast the event.

Closing Ceremony

A unifying close with collective commitments, a group photo, and the official summit pledge — sending 200+ young people home energised and ready to act.
